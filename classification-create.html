Classification System - Create

Download .pdf


For this assignment, I developed a system to classify tabletop miniatures by stage of completion. This system was built around my personal hobby collection, which includes miniatures of various sizes for a variety of tabletop games, and from which I provided examples. As a linear progression of mutually exclusive stages - from unassembled to fully painted - this turned out to be an appropriate case for such a system. As an added challenge, I modeled my categories after Ranganathan's Essential Categories: Personality (Game System), Matter (Size), Energy (Level of Completion), Space (Current Location), and Time (Time Since Purchased).

Based on peer review and my own reflection, I made a number of revisions and refinements to this system. Facets were reordered to reflect user priority, emphasizing level of completion and time since acquisition. For this reason, level of completion was also significantly expanded to offer more granularity, with a new "basing" isolate (the decoration of miniature bases using fake dirt, plants, and the like) and ten sub-isolates reflecting various substages of the process. Isolates for the “Current Location” facet were reordered to suggest a linear progression from the storage box, then to the workbench, then to the display shelf. Based on feedback, the language of the "Time Since Purchased" and "Size" categories and their isolates was also revised for clarity.
