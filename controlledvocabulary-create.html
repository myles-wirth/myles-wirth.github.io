Controlled Vocabulary - Create

For this assignment, I created a thesaurus of episode themes for Star Trek: Deep Space 9. For practicality, I limited the scope to the show's third season. Themes ranged from broad concepts and narrative devices (e.g. "Intrigue", "Anomalous Phenomena") to specific recurring subplots and events (e.g. "Bajoran-Cardassian War"). I applied the vocabulary to several episodes as examples.

This process led to some interesting thematic discoveries, as seemingly unrelated episodes turned out to have thematic similarities. I found the theme of "Doppelgangers" to be significant in a wide variety of contexts across the season, such as shapeshifters, holograms, disguises, and representations of characters appearing in dreams. I enjoyed the messy process of judging these terms' relationality based on the many ways themes overlapped (or didn't) in different episodes. I deemed "Hijinks" to be related to "Embezzlement" and "Racketeering", crimes that appear exclusively in humorous contexts, but not to their broader category "Crime", as that also contained "Murder" - decidedly non-hijinksian in its presentation. Hijinks was also related to themes like "Friendship" which (usually) featured no criminal dimension.

Peer reviews were particularly useful in this assignment to help identify missing terms and broken relationship pairs, as the intricacy of manually constructing a thesaurus lends itself to errors. Feedback was also useful in identifying terms that could benefit from scope notes, either for clarity (Anomalous Phenomena, Doppelgangers) or background information (Bajoran-Cardassian War, Maquis-Cardassian Conflicts).
